Hum

Listens to a room. Reads its background level and the shape of what's in it — steady mains hum, a whine, a low rumble — as a glass sphere that glows gold when quiet and deepens to plum as the room gets louder, its hum fingerprint burning inside.

Levels are relative, not calibrated dB.
